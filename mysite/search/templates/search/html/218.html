E2I

\r
The Center for Engineering Education Innovation (E2I) at The Hong Kong University of Science & Technology enhances student and faculty development by drawing on the latest findings in engineering education research.\r


\r
This website serves as a repository for teaching and learning resources developed by E2I and other international centers.\r




News & Events




2018 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition


September 27, 2018, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
Sponsored by the Center for Engineering Education Innovation, the Champion of the 2018 HKUST 3MT Competition Melody Chung, Bioengineering MPhil student, attended the 2018 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition in Brisbane, Australia on September 27, 2018.\r




2018 HKUST Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Competition


March 27, 2018, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
After hosting the SENG Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Competition for four years the Center for Engineering Education Innovation (E2I) collaborated with the Office of Postgraduate Studies (PGSO) and hosted the first HKUST 3MT Competition on March 27, 2018. \r






2017 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition


September 29, 2017, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
Sponsored by the Center for Engineering Education Innovation, the Champion of the 2017 SENG 3MT Competition Teng Feng (Andrea), Chemical and Biological Engineering PhD student, competed in the 2017 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition, along with 54 contestants from 54 universities in the Asia Pacific Region.\r




How to Critically Read Research Papers and Conduct Peer Review for International Journals


April 27, 2017, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
Invited by the Center for Engineering Education Innovation, Dr Trevor Lane from Edanz Group visited the University and gave a lecture on How to Critically Read Research Papers and Conduct Peer Review for International Journals. \r






2017 SENG Three Minute Thesis Competition


March 22, 2017, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
On March 22, 2017, the Center for Engineering Education Innovation held the SENG Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Competition for the fourth time. After an hour and halfs competition, Teng Feng (Andrea), Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering PhD student under the supervision of Prof Ying Chau, won the championship.\r




2016 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition


September 30, 2016, posted in: Postgraduate, professional development, communication skills

\r
Sponsored by the Center for Engineering Education Innovation, Miss Liwen Jing and Dr Feng Ni, the Champion and the 1st Runner-up of the 2016 SENG Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Competition, participated in the 2016 Asia-Pacific 3MT Competition that was held in the University of Queensland, Brisbane, Australia on September 30, 2016.\r

PMP Activites




Peer Mentors Celebrate Christmas at the End of the Fall Semester


December 21, 2015, posted in: Undergraduate,PMP

\r
The Center for Engineering Education Innovation (E2I) invited the 2015-16 Peer Mentors to a Christmas Gathering on 21 Dec to review and celebrate their efforts in the Peer Mentoring Program during the past Term.\r




Undergraduates Engineered Rafts to Compete in Exciting Rafting Competition


October 1, 2015, posted in: PMP, Undergraduate, Rafting

\r
SENG undergraduates formed teams on their own to design a raft with common materials like plastic water jars, plastic tubes and foam pool noodles.\r






SENGs First-year Students Joined in Engaging Team Building Camp


August 29, 2015, posted in: PMP, Undergraduate

\r
Newly admitted engineering students all gathered for this years Team Building Camp. Students engaged in different team building activities throughout the whole day event.\r




Christmas Gathering for Peer Mentors


December 23, 2014, posted in: PMP, Undergraduate

\r
Peer Mentors gathered to celebrate the Christmas holiday in the Engineering Commons on 23 Dec. Participants went through fun games that challenged their team building skills, discussed their thoughts on the program and their mentoring experiences in the Fall Semester.\r
