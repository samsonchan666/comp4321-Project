School of Science


School of Science (SSCI)

The School of Science is committed to providing a high-quality, balanced education that emphasizes competence, flexibility and creativity. Throughout the Schools four-year curriculum, students are equipped with essential skills, knowledge and confidence to become leaders and independent thinkers. The School currently has five Departments/Divisions which are Life Science (LIFS), Chemistry (CHEM), Mathematics (MATH), Ocean Science (OCES), and Physics (PHYS). The School offers ten BSc degree programs.


Know more here!

School of Engineering


School of Engineering (SENG)

The School of Engineering is HKUSTs largest School. The School enrolls almost 40% of the Universitys student body. It is devoted to advancing knowledge through research, to stimulating learning through teaching, and to promoting the economic and social development of Hong Kong. The School comprises six Departments which are Chemical and Biological Engineering (CBE), Civil and Environmental Engineering (CIVE), Computer Science and Engineering (COMP), Electronic and Computer Engineering (ELEC), Industrial Engineering and Decision Analytics (IEDA) and Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering (MAE). The School offers sixteen engineering degrees.


Know more here!

School of Business and Management


School of Business and Management (SBM)

HKUSTs School of Business and Management was the first business school in the region to earn double accreditation by prestigious Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB) in U.S. and the European Quality Improvement System (EQUIS) in Europe. Since its beginning, the HKUST Business School has been a global leader in business education. The School strives to advance global business knowledge, to nurture business leaders, and to contribute to the economic and social advancement of both Hong Kong and Asia. There are six departments under HKUST Business School, which are Accounting (ACCT), Economics (ECON), Finance (FINA), Information Systems, Business Statistics and Operations Management (ISOM), Management (MGMT), and Marketing (MARK). The School currently offers fourteen BBA or BSc degree programs.


Know more here!

School of Humanities and Social Science


School of Humanities and Social Science (SHSS)

HKUSTs School of Humanities and Social Science is the largest interdisciplinary university-level humanities and social sciences programs in Greater China, and it is particularly proud of the depth and breadth of its program-wide focus on China. The School has developed strategic partnerships with world renowned universities such as the California Institute of Technology, UCLA and Peking University. The SHSS comprises the Division of Humanities and the Division of Social Science. It offers two BSc degree program.

Know more here!

Interdisciplinary Programs Office


Interdisciplinary Programs Office (IPO)

HKUSTs Interdisciplinary Programs Office is a reflection of the Universitys commitment to innovative teaching, learning and research and to making its degree programs relevant in the modern world. Increasingly, the challenges facing society are multifactorial, multidisciplinary, and often multicultural. Hence, the solutions to global problems come down to management, both of people and of information. The IPO has targeted three specific areas where management is key: environmental health, business risk, and technology, and it offers a degree program in each of these areas.


Know more here!

Stage 2 - Prepare to apply

  1. Check your eligibility for admission to the universities

    Most of the universities accept a wide range of international qualifications. These include but are not limited to HKDSE, IB, GCEAL and SAT. Some universities would have language proficiency or subject requirements. You can find the admission requirements of different universities on their admission websites. HKUST welcomes students from different backgrounds; please check the Admissions page for details of our admission requirements of various recognized qualifications. Also, check out International Qualifications pageto find out more.
  2. Dont miss the application deadline

    Different universities have different application deadlines. The deadlines would be different for local/international curriculum as well.

    HKUST has different application deadlines for applicants with different qualifications;
  • HKDSE: Follow JUPAS timeline
  • International Qualifications / Post-Secondary Qualifications: 15th November 2018 (Early Round), 7th January 2019 (Main Round)
  • Mainland JEE: 11 June 2019

Stage 3 - Apply

Follow application procedures and submit application with required documents

Some countries or regions may have centralized application process for applying to higher education, such as JUPAS in Hong Kong, UCAS in UK, and the Common Application in US. Please check the application channel of your interested universities.

If you are interested in pursuing studies in HKUST, your journey starts by creating an account at the HKUST Online Application System or registering at Joint University Programmes Admissions System (JUPAS) (For students taking HKDSE ONLY). Please also visit Admissions pageto know more about your application procedure according to the type of curriculum you are taking.

Stage 4 - Wait for offer

Pressing the Submit button does not mean the application process is completed.

  • Ensure all the required documents are sent to the admission office/faculty
  • Update the latest examination results once you receive them
  • Check your email or mail box regularly as you will receive update or interview invitation from the university via email

You may upload documents, update exam results and view the status via the HKUST online application system

Stage 5 - Receive the offer

Congratulations! Your journey to university is about to begin.

If you receive more than one offer, give yourself a round of applause. You may want to ponder what you look for in university education and pick your dream choice. Do not miss the offer acceptance deadline and you may now start preparing for your university life.

After you have accepted the offer, the University will then inform you the arrangement on hall application, and course registration etc.

In addition, HKUST provides all-round support to help new students to adapt to university life. You may check out the First Year Success webpage to prepare yourself.

Enjoy your university life

Most of the universities would offer student orientation or special arrangements to help freshmen to adapt to university life. Learn more about the university community, study requirement, supports from the university or the departments. Most importantly, get to know more people, many of them will become your friends.

In HKUST, there are lots of orientation activities and more than 120 student societies for new students to join. Check out the Campus Life page to get a feel of student life in HKUST. You can also find out more on Experiential Learning page about the opportunities that HKUST offers to new students.
